## 3. The Hardware/Software Interface

**Source:** *Computer Organization and Design: The Hardware/Software Interface*

With roughly five years of hands-on software experience but comparatively limited hardware exposure (apart from Day 1's readings), this topic opened up the layer that bridges the two — previously unexplored territory.

### Classifications of Computing Systems

- **Personal Mobile Devices (PMDs):** PCs, smartphones, and similar consumer-facing devices
- **Servers:** Systems optimized for specific applications, typically accessed over a network
- **Embedded Systems:** The most ubiquitous category, found in washing machines, televisions, smart door locks, and similar everyday devices

### Historical Optimization Priorities

The chapter outlined how engineering priorities have shifted across computing generations:

| Era | Primary Optimization Focus |
|---|---|
| Earlier generations | Memory size allocation |
| Later generations | Power and processing allocation |
| Current generation | Layered memory management and core allocation |

Modern developers are expected to understand how layered memory architecture works and how to leverage core allocation effectively to complete computational tasks efficiently.
